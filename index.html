About Me

Kefan is currently a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Auburn University. He is advised by Dr. Shiwen Mao and co-advised by Dr. Jitendra K. Tugnait. His research focuses on TCP and video streaming performance over wireless networks, applying techniques such as machine learning, deep learning, optimization, and control theory.

Research Projects

  • Dynamic adaptive streaming over HTTP (DASH) performance over OFDM networks and next generation networks.
  • TCP congestion control over wireless networks.

Projects

FMCW/CW based Mobile Phone Sonar Radar and its application on gesture tracking

  • Built with Android Studio and Java and tested on Nexus 6P.
  • Fine-grained movement direction and distance are obtained from frequency and phase variation.
  • Achieved 2-D mm level precision for objects with cm2 level area like fingers.

Deep Learning based Indoor localization using wireless CSI Implementation and Testing

  • Developed based on Tensorflow and Python.
  • Implemented and tested deep restricted Boltzmann machine (RBM) with tensorflow for wireless localization
  • Data measured from Intel WiFi Link 5300 was utilized and achieved 0.5m precision
